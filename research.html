Research

Understanding human development through stem cell biology

Research Overview

We study how cells build the human body. We use human pluripotent stem cells to build organoids that recapitulate essential features of developmental processes. With these simplified in vitro models, we dive deep into mechanisms that integrate gene regulation, cellular dynamics, and tissue-level cues to precisely pattern the body plan.

Research Areas

Somite Image

Somite Development

We focus on somites in the early embryo that give rise to our spine. We combine live imaging, functional genomics, and cell engineering to uncover fundamental principles of multicellular patterning and morphogenesis.

Limb Bud Image

Limb Bud Formation

Our research on limb buds explores how these structures develop into our limbs. We investigate the cellular and molecular mechanisms that drive limb patterning and growth.

Organoid Image

Organoid Engineering

We build organoids that recapitulate essential features of developmental processes. These simplified in vitro models help us understand complex biological systems.

Research Goals

Advance Human Developmental Biology

Understanding the fundamental principles that govern human development and tissue formation.

Understand Congenital Diseases

Using our models to study developmental disorders and identify potential therapeutic targets.

Promote Regenerative Medicine

Leveraging our knowledge to advance tissue and organ engineering for medical applications.

Research Methods

Live Imaging

Real-time observation of cellular processes and tissue development.

Functional Genomics

Comprehensive analysis of gene function and regulation during development.

Cell Engineering

Manipulation and modification of cells to study specific biological processes.

Organoid Culture

Three-dimensional cell culture systems that mimic organ development.
